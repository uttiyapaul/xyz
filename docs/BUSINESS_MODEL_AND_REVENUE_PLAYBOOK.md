# Business Model And Revenue Playbook

Last updated: 2026-03-16

## Purpose

This is the living business, GTM, monetization, and product-commercialization
document for the platform.

Why this file exists:
- It keeps product development tied to business outcomes, not only feature
  completeness.
- It captures commercialization ideas discovered while building the portal so
  they are not lost in chat history or personal notes.
- It gives founders, operators, sales, partnerships, investor-facing teams, and
  future agents one place to understand what to sell, to whom, why it matters,
  and what product gaps still block revenue expansion.

Important note:
- This document is a strategic working draft, not a legal promise, pricing
  commitment, or market-validated final policy.
- Anything that affects regulation, assurance, independence, or contractual
  obligations still needs legal, finance, and operations review before being
  sold as binding product behavior.

## Core Business Model

### What The Platform Sells

The business is not only "GHG calculation software."

It can become a multi-layer climate operations business with recurring software
revenue, implementation revenue, managed reporting revenue, assurance workflow
revenue, and later market/intelligence revenue.

Primary value stack:
1. Measure emissions.
2. Operationalize reporting and evidence.
3. Prepare disclosures and CBAM outputs.
4. Coordinate review, approval, and verifier workflows.
5. Create decision-grade finance and executive reporting.
6. Add consulting, audit orchestration, digital twin, and carbon-market
   services as higher-margin expansion layers.

### Product Layers

1. Core operating system
- GHG source register
- activity capture
- readings
- documents and evidence
- submissions and signoff chain

2. Compliance layer
- framework disclosures
- CBAM declarations
- filing readiness
- privacy and governance controls

3. Assurance layer
- reviewer and approver queues
- verifier RFI, sampling, and vault workflows
- independence and signoff integrity posture

4. Finance layer
- carbon liability view
- curated board packs
- offsets and credit-market support

5. Strategic layer
- targets
- group consolidation
- AI validation
- digital twin and scenarios

6. Service layer
- implementation
- managed reporting
- verifier sourcing/orchestration
- consulting
- sector templates
- regulator/government reporting programs

### Segment A: Small Shop / Small Business / Small Facility

Example:
- one factory
- one warehouse
- one processing unit
- one export line

- keep setup simple

Why it helps them:

## Can Government Be A Client?

Yes, but government is likely a separate business motion, not the same motion
as private-enterprise sales.

### Government / Public Sector Client Types

1. Ministry or regulator
- reporting portal
- sector dashboards
- compliance intake
- audit oversight surfaces

2. PSU / state-owned enterprise
- similar to enterprise buyer
- large multi-site footprint
- strong governance and reporting pressure

3. Industrial development authority
- cluster-level emissions reporting
- supplier readiness programs
- export and compliance readiness programs

4. Smart city / municipal / public infrastructure
- fleet, facilities, utilities, and disclosure operations

### What To Sell To Government

- regulated reporting portal
- evidence and audit trail system
- sector readiness dashboards
- cluster or scheme reporting
- state-level exporter readiness program

### Government-Specific Cautions

- procurement cycles are slower
- security, hosting, residency, and audit requirements are stricter
- pricing and delivery model may need a separate public-sector edition

## Audit / Verifier Revenue Model Ideas

### Option 1: Client Appoints Auditor Themselves

Pros:
- preserves strong independence optics
- simpler commercial structure

Cons:
- platform may lose coordination revenue
- onboarding friction remains with the client

Best when:
- enterprise clients already have assurance partners

### Option 2: Platform Recommends Orchestrated Auditor Network

Pros:
- easier client experience
- new partner/referral revenue
- faster assurance setup

Cons:
- must preserve independence boundaries carefully
- must avoid appearing to compromise assurance neutrality

Potential model:
- client selects from approved verifier marketplace
- platform charges workflow/orchestration fee, not opinion outcome fee
- verifier engagement and independence declarations remain explicit

### Option 3: Managed Readiness Before Auditor Starts

What you sell:
- pre-assurance cleanup
- evidence pack preparation
- workflow hardening
- RFI readiness

Why it is attractive:
- high-value service
- easier to justify commercially
- lowers verifier friction without directly selling final assurance

Recommended stance:
- do not own the assurance opinion commercially in a way that creates
  independence concerns
- do sell readiness, workflow, marketplace matching, and evidence preparation
