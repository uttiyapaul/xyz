FAQ

Questions we get asked before every demo

What is CBAM and when does it apply to us?
CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism) is the EU regulation requiring importers of steel, cement, aluminium, fertilisers, hydrogen, and electricity to surrender CBAM certificates for embedded carbon in their products from 2026. If your company exports these goods to the EU, or is a supplier to companies that do, you must quantify and report embedded emissions now. Transitional reporting started in October 2023.
Do we need ISO 14064 certification to use the platform?
No certification is required to use A2Z GHG Platform. The platform is designed to help you work toward ISO 14064 compliance, with built-in verification workflows, emission factor libraries, and audit trails that meet the standard's requirements. Many clients use A2Z as their path to first certification rather than a post-certification tool.
How does the amine supply business connect to the platform?
Uniquely — when you install a carbon capture system using A2Z amine products, the captured CO₂ quantity is automatically fed into your GHG platform inventory, reducing your reported Scope 1 total. This closes the loop: the same platform that quantifies your emissions also accounts for the carbon you physically remove. No double-counting, fully verifiable, ready for CBAM certificate offset claims.
How long does it take to onboard a multi-site conglomerate?
For a 4–6 site conglomerate with existing ERP data, typical onboarding takes 4–8 weeks. This includes data migration, ERP connector setup, emission factor assignment, facility boundary definition, and first Scope 1 inventory sign-off. Complex multi-jurisdiction setups (e.g. Indian + EU facilities) take 10–12 weeks.
What makes your CCS project service different from a regular EPC contractor?
Three things: first, we supply our own amine products — eliminating the solvent procurement risk that causes 70% of CCS operating cost overruns. Second, we integrate capture data directly into the GHG platform for immediate MRV (Measurement, Reporting, Verification) without a third-party data handoff. Third, we have in-house carbon credit brokerage to monetise the captured CO₂ from day one of operations.
Is my data secure and GDPR compliant?
Yes. The platform is hosted on ISO 27001-aligned infrastructure with data encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Our Data Processing Agreement is available for EU customers. All audit logs are cryptographically signed and tamper-evident. We are currently pursuing SOC 2 Type II certification. Full GDPR compliance including right to access, deletion, and portability is built in.
Do you supply MEA amine in India? What are your lead times?
Yes — we supply MEA, DEA, MDEA, and custom blends across India via IBC (1,000 litre) and ISO-tank. Standard grades: 5–10 business day lead time from order. Custom blends: 3–6 weeks depending on formulation complexity. We also maintain buffer inventory for key accounts on annual supply agreements. Pan-India logistics covered.
Can the platform handle Indian numbering (lakhs / crores)?
Yes — the platform fully supports Indian locale formatting including lakhs and crores for financial figures, INR currency display, and Indian date formats. European users see standard metric formatting. All underlying data is stored in SI units (tCO₂e, MWh, GJ) regardless of display locale.
