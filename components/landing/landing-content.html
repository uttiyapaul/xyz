How amine scrubbing works: Flue gas from combustion is passed through an amine solvent column. CO₂ reacts selectively with the amine (e.g. MEA forms a carbamate), separating it from N₂ and H₂O. The rich solvent is regenerated in a stripper, releasing pure CO₂ for storage or utilisation, while the amine is recycled back into the absorber.

FAQ

Common questions

What is CBAM and when does it apply to us?
The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism requires exporters of steel, cement, aluminium, fertilisers, hydrogen, and electricity to the EU to declare the embedded carbon emissions in their products. From January 2026, CBAM certificates must be purchased for the carbon that isn't priced in the country of origin. By 2034 the full carbon cost applies. If you export to the EU in these sectors, you are affected now.
How is your amine different from what I can buy from a commodity supplier?
Commodity amine is sold at technical grade, which carries higher impurities (degradation products, heat-stable salts) that foul your absorber faster and increase your solvent make-up costs. Our amines are process-grade with Certificate of Analysis on every batch. We also offer long-term supply agreements with price stability — critical for CCS project economics that span 15–20 years.
Can I use the platform without your amine or CCS services?
Absolutely. The GHG platform is a standalone SaaS product. You can track emissions, generate CBAM declarations, manage BRSR filings, and give verifier access — all without needing our amine or project services. The Full Carbon Stack is an option, not a requirement.
How long does a carbon capture project take to deliver?
For a standard post-combustion retrofit on an existing industrial flue gas stack, the typical timeline is 12–24 months from feasibility study sign-off to first CO₂ captured. Modular DAC units can be installed in 6–12 months. Greenfield CCS integrated into new plant construction is 24–36 months from FEED.
Does the platform integrate with SAP or Oracle?
Yes. We have pre-built connectors for SAP S/4HANA (PM, MM, and Energy Management modules) and Oracle Fusion. For other ERPs, we offer a flat-file API and a webhook-based integration layer. Most enterprise integrations go live within 4–6 weeks of project kick-off.
Is my emission data secure and private?
Yes. All data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Each organisation's data is logically isolated in separate database schemas. We never use one client's data to train models or inform another client's benchmarks. Role-based access control means your data is only visible to the users you explicitly authorise.
Can we generate carbon credits from our CCS project?
Yes, if your capture is independently verified. We handle the full MRV cycle from capture measurement through third-party verification to credit issuance on Verra VCS, Gold Standard, or sovereign registries. We can also facilitate offtake agreements with corporate buyers if you want to monetise the credits rather than retire them against your own net-zero targets.
How does your BRSR reporting work for SEBI compliance?
The platform auto-generates the BRSR Core Principle 6 (Environmental) section including P6-E1 through E9 from your underlying emission data. The output is a structured BRSR-format report that can be submitted directly to your board and included in the Annual Report. We also support BRSR Reasonable Assurance (Category B) for companies that need third-party verification.
